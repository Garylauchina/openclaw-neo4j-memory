## What Phase 4 established

Phase 4 established that the lightweight regrowth line can grow from a note-quality layer into a stronger reusable memory-product layer.

Specifically, it established a product layer that is:
- more reusable,
- more role-aware,
- more evidence-layered,
- more useful under retrieval,
- and more inspectable.

## What remained intentionally out of scope

Phase 4 still did not reopen:
- root axiom systems
- structural debt systems
- self-modification protocols
- crystal generation/transfer implementation
- adapter systems
- operating-system style governance layers
- predictive prophylaxis systems
- full conflict arbitration kernels
- autonomous self-evolution controllers
- large governance shells around product quality

## Why this freeze matters

This freeze preserves a fourth proof point:
- the new regrowth line can now produce a clearer reusable product layer,
- without regrowing the earlier heavy architecture.

This makes Phase 4 a reusable baseline for:
- Phase 5 planning
- comparison against both Phase 3 and the older heavy baseline line
- detecting future inflation if later phases begin to overgrow routing/governance or abstraction too early

## Rule after freeze

After this freeze:
- Phase 4 should not be broadened by quietly introducing governance-heavy or routing-heavy vocabulary under product-language disguises
- future additions should be justified by clear Phase 5 scope
- if later work starts drifting into axiom/debt/kernel/governance-shell language, compare back to this freeze before continuing

## One-sentence freeze statement

Phase 4 is frozen as the fourth successful lightweight regrowth stage: a more reusable, role-aware, evidence-layered, retrieval-useful, and inspectable memory-product layer, intentionally preserved before stronger routing or any carefully bounded governance layer begins.

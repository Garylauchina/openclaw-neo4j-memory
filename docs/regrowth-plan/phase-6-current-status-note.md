# Phase 6 Current Status Note

## Current status

Phase 6 is currently:

## scoped, cataloged, and under evidence gathering,
## but not admitted into intervention implementation

This means:
- scope exists
- merge breakdown exists
- working boundary exists
- failure-case catalog exists
- entry decision exists
- evidence collection plan exists
- evidence log exists
- but governance intervention merges have not been admitted

## Why Phase 6 remains paused

Phase 6 was explicitly designed to allow governance only if concrete repeated failure pressure clearly justifies it.

Current evidence does not yet meet that threshold.

The current evidence log contains early candidate examples for:
- FC-1 product/raw-memory imbalance
- FC-2 product vs fresh raw evidence tension
- FC-3 mode-specific routing degradation
- FC-4 recurrent ambiguous selection

But all currently recorded examples remain weak or unclear rather than governance-justifying.

## Current judgment

At this stage:
- governance pressure is conceivable,
- governance pressure is cataloged,
- governance pressure is being monitored,
- but governance pressure is not yet sufficiently demonstrated.

Therefore:

## Phase 6 should remain paused after Merge 1

## What would change the status

Phase 6 may proceed beyond Merge 1 only if at least one failure case accumulates:
- repeated concrete examples,
- visible degradation,
- and a clear argument that bounded intervention is smaller and cleaner than continued routing/product tuning.

If those conditions are not met, Phase 6 should remain in evidence-gathering mode.

## What should not happen now

The project should not now:
- implement intervention rules speculatively
- widen governance because the architecture looks ready
- create broader policy or rule-engine structures
- use weak examples as justification for Merge 2

## Practical recommendation

The correct short-term stance is:
- keep Phase 6 paused,
- keep gathering concrete evidence only when useful,
- and continue relying on the current lightweight write/product/routing stack unless stronger failure pressure emerges.

## One-sentence status statement

Phase 6 is currently paused in evidence-gathering mode: governance remains possible in principle, but not yet justified in practice.
